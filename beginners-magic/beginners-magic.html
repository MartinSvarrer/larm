A beginners guide to Magic the Gathering

This is not as much a guide for beginners, as it's me, a beginner, attempting to make small guides for other beginners.

This is not a guide to learn the game, but more about all the scary, confusing things.

  • Buying cards
  • Multiple ways to play the game

Buying cards

Get a starter kit.

A starter kit is basically all you need to play. Like buying a board game.

Each starter kit comes with 2 decks.

Don't worry about getting the newest kit or if the starter kit comes with a special theme, like Lord of the Rings.

Buying more cards (booster packs)

When you buy Magic the gathering cards, they come in "booster packs".

Booster pack just means a bunch of random cards.

There is different types of boosters available: Draft, set, jumpstart and collector boosters (there is probably more, that I don't know about).

Draft boosters

Draft boosters are the original type of boosters, and works well for playing the draft and sealed game formats (game formats is different ways you can play the game).

I haven't actually tried to play draft format myself yet. But I did buy some draft boosters, just to own some cards, that wasn't part of a deck (didn't want to split my starter deck).

I think I had the most fun with buying some Dominaria Remastered draft boosters because they have some old cards reprinted in their original design, which to me looks super cool.

Set boosters

I also bought some set boosters to own even more cards. Unlike draft boosters, they don't have a special purpose other than just opening packs.

Right now I prefer to buy set boosters, if I just want to open some packs of cards.

Jumpstart boosters

Jumpstart boosters are pre-made decks around a single theme.

You need two packs per player. You likely want to keep these cards together to play with them again.

I ended up buying some Dragon Shield cube shell's to store my jumpstart decks in (fits 20 cards perfectly).

Whenever I want to play jumpstart I take four random boxes, shuffle two together for each player, and we play.

I often play with the same combo a few times, to get an understanding of the cards in the deck better.

I have found that you don't often see the same cards game to game, in this format.

I didn't try to buy any collector boosters yet.

Game formats

I played with the starter decks. Great fun. Don't know what format they are. Doesn't matter.

I also played jumpstart a bunch. Its a great way to try and play some more cards, without having to build any decks.
